## Introduction

Remember when cutting the cord meant choosing between Netflix and Hulu? Those days are long gone. In 2026, cord-cutting has evolved into a full-fledged entertainment revolution, with more options, better hardware, and bigger savings than ever before.
But here's the thing: the cable industry still rakes in billions from subscribers who haven't made the switch. Why? Because cutting the cord can feel overwhelming. Between streaming devices, subscription services, antennas, and privacy concerns, it's easy to see why some people stick with that $150 monthly cable bill out of sheer inertia.

That ends today.

**What you'll save:** The average American household pays $157 per month for cable television. A well-planned streaming setup costs $50-80 monthly, including internet. That's $900-1,200 back in your pocket annually.

## Streaming Services Breakdown

### Premium On-Demand Services

**Netflix** - The original streaming giant still leads in original content. $15.49-22.99/month

**Max** - HBO's premium library plus Warner Bros. movies. $15.99-19.99/month

**Disney+** - Disney, Marvel, Star Wars, Pixar. $13.99/month

**Hulu** - Next-day access to current TV episodes. $7.99-17.99/month

**Bundle tip:** The Disney Bundle (Disney+, Hulu, ESPN+) for $14.99/month is one of the best values in streaming.

### Live TV Streaming Services

**YouTube TV** - $72.99/month, 100+ channels. Best for full cable replacement.

**Hulu + Live TV** - $76.99/month including Disney+, ESPN+. Best for Disney bundle fans.

**Sling TV** - $40-55/month. Best for budget-conscious live TV viewers.
